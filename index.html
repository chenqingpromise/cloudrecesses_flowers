Cloud Recesses

☁ The residence of the Lan Sect was located in a remote mountain outside the city of Gusu.

Mist constantly enveloped the white walls and black roofs of the buildings, which stretched along the picturesque garden of the waterside pavilion, as if it was an ocean of clouds in the immortal realm. At dawn, the first rays of the morning sun shone through the hazy billows of mist that drifted far and wide, perfectly complementing its name, the “Cloud Recesses”.

☁ In the middle of blue and white posters.

The furniture in the jingshi was extremely simplistic, without any unnecessary belongings. On the accordion partition, there was a painting of drifting clouds, floating and morphing with its fine brushwork.

☁ Hanguang-jun melody.

A guqin table lay horizontally in front of it. On top of the three-legged incense stand in the corner, a hollowed out incense burner made of white jade emitted soft, lingering smoke, filling the whole room with the chilling scent of sandalwood.

☁ A bell rang every evening at nine, to remind the disciples to sleep between the hours of nine pm and five am.

The name of the place is derived from a line in the poet Jia Dao's "Visiting the Absent Hermit": 松下问童子，言师采药去, 只在此山中，云深不知处 – (When I questioned your pupil under a pine tree, he answered, "My teacher went to get herbs." But how can I tell toward which corner of the mountain, through all these clouds?) The Chinese name actually translates to "Somewhere unknown deep in the clouds."
